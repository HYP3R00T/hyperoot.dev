I have always imagined life as a walk in a dense forest. How it started is still a blur, but as much as I remember, I am walking down a trail. The morning fog was as dense as the forest. I was feeling refreshed, as if I had just been reborn. For a couple of hours, I was enjoying that ambience. I was feeling the earth beneath me. I listened to the music caused by the winds sprinting through the trees. It was beautiful.

After walking for a few hours more, I began to feel alone. There was no one to talk to. I was surrounded by life, the floras and faunas. But I felt empty. Going back was not an option because I didn’t know where did I start. All I could do was keep on walking. Hours turned into days, and I had not seen a single soul till then. I would walk during the day and sleep during the night in a small tent with a kindling fire nearby. There was a streamlet nearby. Food and water were in ample supply.

One fine day, I was walking down the trail when I noticed another trail merging into mine. A spark of hope was ignited. I was hoping someone would walk down that trail, and maybe I would finally find the companionship I had been craving for days. So, I camped over there and waited for someone to come. Luckily, on the evening of the next day, a girl came. She seemed slightly startled by my presence but relieved to know she was not alone. Like every evening, I lit a small fire and put up my tent. She was tired. I got her some food, and we sat around that small fire.

She told me about her journey. I shared my experiences. I expressed my excitement to have finally met someone. We were lying down and peeking at the stars through trees that night. We shared many tales, myths, legends and stories. I wished for the night not to be over for once. It was just captivating to have someone to talk to. But time awaits none. The next day, we resumed our journey.

This time, everything was different. The presence of someone walking next to me was blissful. Soon, we became friends. We started caring for each other, and slowly, that care morphed into love. It was like an adventure. I started noticing small things. I noticed how she looked at me and smiled when I was not looking at her. I would look into her eyes while her eyes would chase a butterfly. Those moments were gorgeous.

But eventually, we had some fights, some disagreements. It started with small things but contorted into something worse. We grew bitter. So, one day, we decided to go our separate ways. A split came on the trail. She went into one, and I went into another, never to be seen again. I felt terrible, but it was too late. I continued that path for many days. While I hoped I would never meet anyone else, I craved the warmth of someone’s presence.

And again, I found someone. I was sceptical this time. How do I avoid those old unpleasantries? What could I do not cause the same outcome? This time also, I felt the love and someone’s presence. Again, I fell in love, and things seemed better. Maybe it was destiny or something I said or did, but she also went away. Down that trail, I met many people and had a fallout with them. There were moments when I wanted to go back and change everything, but it was no longer an option. We, all, had moved on. I enjoyed their company for as long as they were there and carried their memories for the rest of the journey.

Why am I sharing all of these? There’s no moral to learn. There’s no ending. This path down the trail across all kinds of horizons is how I see our lives. We all are walking on some trail, never to return. Our paths may sometimes cross, and we might get to know each other well. Now and then, we will stop and take a moment to think about it all. We would share our pain and pleasure.

But life goes on. The trail will never end. We will never know where we came from and where we are going. Every step I took was a surprise. I never knew what life would bring. There were days when I saw rainbows. There were the darkest of nights as well. There were moments when I hated myself so much. But there were also those moments when I felt like floating in the sky. There were days when I would see the buds bloom into flowers. There were days when I would stomp on fallen petals.

Some days would be good, and some will be bad. But I am glad that I got to walk the path. And when I look back, all I see is a beautiful story, an adventure worth having. And the story doesn’t end here. I am still walking with someone. I am still enjoying the rain. The fog is still there, and I have no idea what comes next.

I have come a long way, but there is a long way to go—things to do and people to meet; I pause and look at life whenever possible. I shall miss those memories and will be prepared for more to come. Ultimately, it all comes down to the journey, not the destination.
